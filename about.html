About Me

My name is Lindsay. I am a student of Information Science with a concentration on Data Science & Analytics and a minor in Creative Writing at the University of South Florida. I was born in Kentucky, but have lived 17 years as a Floridian in Tampa. I have worked for over a decade in many different industries from government contracting to retail sales.

When I was 15 years old, I dropped out of a magnet Arts program in high school. Later, I was diagnosed with ADHD and PTSD. I was working at an auto repair shop when I was laid off during the pandemic. A year later, I found myself laid off again, and decided it was time to go back to school. I completed my GED in four months and began college shortly thereafter. In 2024, I graduated from Hillsborough Community College with an AA in Computer Information Systems and transferred to the University of South Florida to study data science. In May, 2026, I will be graduating summa cum laude with a 3.9 GPA. I am a statistical outlier who studies the practical applications of statistics.

During my time at the University of South Florida, I have maintained an active presence on campus. Since 2024, I have maintained two part-time jobs working at USF and HCC’s tutoring centers as an Academic Assistant and a Writing Tutor. In 2025, I was promoted to Lead Academic Assistant at USF. I have also participated in USF’s Geology, Philosophy, and AI Society clubs, briefly serving as an officer of the AI Society. During the summer of 2025, I volunteered part-time at USF’s RARE robotics lab where I assisted with machine-learning powered HRI (human-robot interaction) research.

I chose to study data science at USF because I have a wide variety of interests and experience, and data science is broadly applicable to nearly every area of life. I am motivated primarily by endless curiosity and the desire to look into a system and discover what is empirically true. I can then use those discoveries to produce results that bring about real impact and change. I feel especially suited to data science because it allows me to combine the powers of evidence, prediction, and storytelling. I am naturally inclined toward both the sciences and communication, which represent equally important aspects of a data scientist’s work.

Throughout the course of my bachelor’s degree, I have focused on sharpening my logic, programming, analysis, and communication skills. I have worked with raw data, relational databases, information gathering and organization, and communicating the results of findings. I have worked with many different types of data, employing statistical models and machine learning algorithms to analyze information across various domains. My minor in Creative Writing also complements my technical background by demonstrating my ability to communicate intelligently and appropriately. As an aspiring data scientist, analysis findings are only as meaningful as they are able to be communicated to bring about action. Together with my analytical and communication skills, I have used tools like Excel, Python, R, SQL, and others to fully evaluate, report, and visualize the findings of analyses I have conducted.

According to general labor statistics from the time I entered my program path in 2022, data and information related careers have been projected to boast above-average demand and growth. They also tend to be associated with competitive salaries and rate highly in life satisfaction scores. Over the past two years, I have submitted over 600 job applications, including for unpaid internship positions, and received zero offers. This is a highly competitive field, and future graduates should be aware of this before deciding on this path. While skills and experience are important, and some entry-level data related roles do exist for those who hold a minimum of a bachelor’s degree, pursuing further education and networking aggressively are unquestionable factors of potential success in this field. My goal after graduation is to find full-time employment that utilizes the skills I learned while pursuing my bachelor’s degree.
